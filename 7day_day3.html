Day 3 — Emotional Awareness

7 Days to Change · $5 Program

Emotions are not problems.
They are signals.

Why Emotional Awareness Matters

Most people think they are reacting to life. In truth, they are reacting to emotions they never noticed.

Living upstream means learning to feel without drowning, and listen without being controlled.

Emotion vs Story

Every emotion has two parts:

  • The physical sensation in the body
  • The mental story layered on top

Today, we separate the two.

  • Tight chest
  • Heavy stomach
  • Jaw tension
  • Restlessness
  • Numbness

Sensations are neutral. The suffering comes from resisting them.

Real-Life Example

Someone feels anger and immediately believes, “I’m being disrespected.”

Day 3 teaches you to pause and ask: “What does this feel like in my body — before the story?”

Exercise — Naming the Feeling

  1. When an emotion arises, pause.
  2. Locate it in your body.
  3. Name it simply: anger, sadness, fear, joy, tension.
  4. Breathe into the sensation for 10 seconds.

Naming creates space. Space creates choice.

Journaling — Emotional Mapping

  • What emotions showed up today?
  • Where did you feel them physically?
  • What story did your mind attach?
  • What changed when you stayed with the feeling?

You are not required to dive deep. Awareness can be gentle.

If an emotion feels too intense, focus on your breath or surroundings instead.

Integration for Today

Today’s guiding question:

“What am I actually feeling right now?”

This question alone can dissolve years of unconscious reaction.
